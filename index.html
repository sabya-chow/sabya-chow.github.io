🔬 CausalAlpha — SMA Hypothesis Testing Framework ● live

A 9-phase causal inference pipeline that answers: does the S&P 500 SMA signal causally drive forward returns, or is the backtest profit confounded by the underlying market regime? Implements naive backtesting, DAG construction, DoWhy identification, and a full estimator battery — Linear Backdoor, IPW, Double ML, X-Learner, Causal Forest — followed by placebo and robustness tests.

🧬 Prostate MRI PI-RADS Predictor ↗ ● live

Physics-aware VAE that predicts PI-RADS 1–5 cancer risk scores from prostate MRI slices (T2, ADC, BVAL). CNN encoder compresses each slice into a latent space used simultaneously for image reconstruction and risk classification — the reconstruction loss acts as a regulariser that forces the latent representation to retain clinically meaningful structure. Built at Uncommon Hacks 2026 (Social Impact Track) with a UChicago team of 4. Deployed on HuggingFace Spaces; trained on NYU fastMRI prostate dataset.

About

I work at the Reserve Bank of India and am currently pursuing an MS in Applied Data Science at the University of Chicago. My research sits at the intersection of causal inference, machine learning, and quantitative finance.

I approach problems through a probabilistic and Bayesian lens — preferring rigorous identification over correlational shortcuts. A recurring theme across my work: distinguishing genuine signal from regime-driven noise.

Outside of research: stochastic calculus, reinforcement learning, earnings call NLP, and Kaggle competitions.
